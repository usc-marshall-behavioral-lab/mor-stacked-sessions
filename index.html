Overview
Collect verified, proctored survey responses through shared lab sessions

Multiple short Qualtrics studies run back-to-back in LL102. Participants complete each study at individual workstations under RA supervision, with study order counterbalanced across days (all sessions within a day run the same order). Participants sign up for a fixed time slot and must remain for its full duration — everyone in the room starts and finishes together. You provide collaborator access to your Qualtrics survey — we handle scheduling, proctoring, SONA setup, and credit assignment.

What you provide

Submit the following five items by email, finalized by the day before your first session:

1. Qualtrics collaborator access — the coordinator needs collaborator access (not just a link) to configure the redirect chain between studies
2. Estimated study duration
3. IRB protocol number
4. Study name
5. Preferred session weeks
What you get
✓ ID-verified participants on dedicated lab computers — tab switching and external applications are disabled
✓ Up to 16 seats per session, 6 sessions per day (~84 participants/day)
✓ Participants are required to remain for the full session duration and are dismissed simultaneously
✓ A brief neutral transition screen between studies reduces carryover and priming effects
✓ Participants who complete your study in a stacked session are automatically excluded from the online SONA version — no risk of duplicate participation
✓ SONA credit: 0.5 (≤ 30 min) · 0.75 (31–45 min) · 1.0 (46–60 min)
✓ Your survey content, logic, and data are untouched — responses appear in your standard Qualtrics export
Eligibility
✓ Qualtrics-based online survey
✓ Individual-level (no groups or dyads)
✓ ≤ 30 minutes in duration
✓ Active IRB approval
✓ No deception

Process after submission
1

Compatibility review

The coordinator checks for participant overlap, potential priming conflicts, and scheduling fit with other studies in the stack.

2

Scheduling & setup

Session weeks are confirmed, the SONA study page is created, and the coordinator configures the Qualtrics redirect chain linking all studies in the stack. No further action is required from the researcher.

3

Data collection

An RA proctors each session. Participants complete all studies in the stack and receive a single SONA credit award based on total session time (0.5, 0.75, or 1.0 credit). Responses appear in the researcher's standard Qualtrics export.

FAQ
Frequently asked questions

Common questions about how sessions work, participant flow, credit, and data quality.

How do participants move between studies — are the surveys linked?
Yes. Studies are connected via a Qualtrics redirect chain: when a participant finishes one study, they are automatically redirected to the next one without returning to SONA in between. The coordinator configures this redirect in the end-of-survey settings of each study — that is the only thing touched in your survey. Your content, logic, display order, and data remain completely unchanged. This is why collaborator access to your Qualtrics survey is required rather than just a link.
What prevents a participant from completing my study twice — once in the lab and once online?
Participants who complete your study in a stacked session are automatically excluded from signing up for the online SONA version. This is handled at the SONA level — once they receive credit for the stacked session study, the online listing is no longer available to them. You can also run stacked sessions and online collection simultaneously without risk of overlap.
Can I submit multiple studies of my own to run in the same session?
Yes. Multiple studies from the same researcher can run back-to-back within the same stack, as long as they are on unrelated topics and the combined duration fits within the session cap. Study order will still be counterbalanced across days. If you have a preference for ordering or position in the stack, mention it at submission and the coordinator will accommodate where possible.
When does my study need to be finalized before a session?
Your Qualtrics survey should be fully finalized — content, logic, and flow — by the day before your first session. This gives the coordinator time to configure the redirect chain, test the full session flow end-to-end, and resolve any issues before participants arrive. Changes to survey content after this point cannot be guaranteed to make it into the session.
Can participants leave early if they finish their studies quickly?
No. Participants sign up for a fixed time slot and are required to remain for its entire duration — all participants are dismissed simultaneously by the RA. This is intentional: early departures would reveal to remaining participants that some studies are shorter than others, potentially introducing demand effects. It also ensures the credit tier accurately reflects time in the lab. Participants who complete all studies before the session ends are asked to remain seated until dismissal.
How much SONA credit do participants receive?
Credit is awarded based on total time in the lab session — not per study. The tiers are: 0.5 credit for sessions up to 30 minutes, 0.75 credit for 31–45 minutes, and 1.0 credit for 46–60 minutes. Participants receive one credit award per session regardless of how many studies are in the stack. The current stack duration determines which tier applies, so adding your study may move the session into a higher credit bracket.
Can a participant in Study A also be in Study B within the same session?
Yes — each participant completes all studies in the stack sequentially and receives a single SONA credit award for the session based on total time in the lab. Studies within the same stack should not measure the same construct to avoid priming effects.
What if my study has exclusion criteria that conflict with another study?
This is reviewed during the compatibility check. If there is an exclusion overlap, the stack composition can be adjusted or the studies can be scheduled in different weeks.
Do I need to modify my study for the proctored setting?
No. Each participant works independently at their own station, and your survey runs as designed in Qualtrics. The RA proctor handles session logistics (seating, timing, credit assignment) but does not interact with the study content.
How is study order determined within a session?
By default, study order is counterbalanced across session days — each day uses a different ordering so that order effects wash out across your full sample. Within a single day, all sessions run in the same order. If you have a specific counterbalancing request (e.g., rotating order within the same day, or a particular position in the stack), please mention it when you submit and the coordinator will do their best to accommodate it.
Can I run stacked sessions alongside online collection for the same study?
Yes. Stacked sessions create an additional in-person collection channel. Your Qualtrics link remains active for online participants as well. You can include a question in your survey to identify which responses came from stacked sessions versus online.
What if there aren't enough sessions available for my study on a given day?
Each day runs 6 sessions. If some sessions are full or unavailable, your study can be scheduled in the remaining open sessions on that day, or moved to another available date. Session dates are listed in the collection planner above.
Should I still include attention checks in a proctored session?
Attention checks remain useful as a data quality measure. However, failure rates in proctored settings are typically lower than in unsupervised online completion, so the effective sample size after exclusions should be closer to the raw N collected.
Where does the ~2 no-shows per session estimate come from?
This is a rough average based on typical SONA session attendance. Actual no-show rates vary by time of day, day of week, and point in the semester (attendance tends to drop near finals). The planner uses this estimate to give you a realistic timeline, but your actual collection pace may be faster or slower.
